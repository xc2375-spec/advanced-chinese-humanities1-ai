Focus on Patterns

Learn through radicals and character clues

1. 刂 and judgment, cutting, punishment

In this lesson, the knife-side component 刂 helps students notice a semantic field related to cutting, separation, punishment, or harsh judgment.

判pànsentence
刑xíngpunishment
刺cìstab / satirize
到dàoarrive
Memory tip: when you see 刂, ask whether the character may involve cutting, dividing, a sharp action, or legal punishment. In this lesson that helps especially with 判 and 刑.
In this lesson, why is 刂 a useful clue in 判 and 刑?

2. 亻 and person-centered roles or treatment

The person radical 亻 often helps you recognize words tied to people, human roles, or the way someone is treated.

俘fúcapture / prisoner
侮wǔinsult
传chuánpass on
付fùpay / hand over
Memory tip: 亻 often points your attention back to human action, human roles, or what happens to a person. That helps with words like 俘虏 and 侮辱.
What is the best reason to notice 亻 in 俘 and 侮?

3. Similar-looking, different-meaning pair: 绩 vs 迹

This lesson contains a very useful comparison pair: 功绩 and 事迹. They sound related, but they do not mean the same thing.

功绩gōngjìachievement
事迹shìjìdeed; life record
成绩chéngjìresult
足迹zújìfootprint
Memory tip: 绩 often connects to results or accomplishments, while 迹 often points to traces, marks, or what someone left behind.
Which explanation best captures the difference between 功绩 and 事迹?

4. Similar-looking, different-meaning pair: 形 vs 刑

Another very useful contrast in this lesson is between 形象 and 刑法 / 死刑.

形xíngform; appearance
刑xíngpunishment
形象xíngxiàngimage
刑法xíngfǎcriminal law
Memory tip: 形 usually points to form, shape, or image. 刑 belongs to punishment and law. Same sound, very different semantic field.
What is the key memory point in the pair 形 and 刑?
