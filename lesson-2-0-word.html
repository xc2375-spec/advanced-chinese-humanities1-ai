Focus on Patterns

Learn through radicals, structure, and meaning networks

1. 心 / 忄 often points to inner state

In this lesson, several words belong to the world of mind, feeling, and inner response.

怀疑huáiyídoubt
思考sīkǎothink deeply
悠然yōuráncalmly, leisurely
灵魂línghúnsoul
Memory tip: when a word belongs to mental life or inner state, try asking: is this about thought, feeling, doubt, calmness, or self-awareness?
What is the best shared learning clue in 怀疑, 思考, and 悠然自得?

2. Dream network: 梦 → 梦见 → 蝴蝶

This lesson is easier to remember when you connect the dream words into a single image network.

梦mèngdream
梦见mèngjiàndream about
蝴蝶húdiébutterfly
神秘shénmìmysterious
Memory tip: picture the scene. A single image or story often makes abstract vocabulary much easier to remember.
Why is it useful to group 梦, 梦见, 蝴蝶, and 神秘 together?

3. Contrast pair: 疑问 ↔ 答案

Some abstract words are easiest to learn in pairs, especially when one points to the problem and the other points to the resolution.

疑问yíwènquestion
答案dá'ànanswer
怀疑huáiyídoubt
肯定kěndìngaffirm / definitely
Memory tip: try building a reasoning chain: question → doubt → think → answer → affirmation.
What is the most useful way to study 疑问 and 答案?

4. Abstract philosophy cluster

Some words in this lesson do not share an obvious radical pattern, but they form a strong conceptual network.

存在cúnzàiexistence
真实zhēnshíreal
主宰zhǔzǎidominate
命运mìngyùnfate
Memory tip: when radicals are less helpful, use a big idea. Here the big question is: what is real, what exists, and what controls life?
What is the strongest learning link among 存在, 真实, 主宰, and 命运?
