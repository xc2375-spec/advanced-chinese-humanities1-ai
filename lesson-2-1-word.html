Focus on Patterns

Learn through meaning networks and contrasts

1. Mind-state cluster

In this lesson, several words belong to the world of inner mental state, attention, and emotional awareness.

回忆huíyìrecall
恍惚huǎnghūin a trance
心不在焉xīnbùzàiyānabsent-minded
心灵xīnlíngmind, heart
Memory tip: when several words describe the inner world, group them as a “mind-state network.” This often helps more than looking for a single radical.
What is the strongest shared learning link among 回忆, 恍惚, 心不在焉, and 心灵?

2. Time and duration pairings

This lesson includes several useful words about continuity, time flow, and duration.

川流不息chuānliúbùxīcontinuous flow
有始有终yǒushǐyǒuzhōngcarry through to the end
漫长mànchángvery long
短促duǎncùvery brief
Memory tip: pairing words about long flow, completion, and short duration builds a strong time-based memory network.
Why is it useful to learn 川流不息, 有始有终, 漫长, and 短促 together?

3. Appearance and perception cluster

Another strong network in this lesson centers on self-image, perception, and what is seen from the outside.

镜子jìngzimirror
面孔miànkǒngface
印象yìnxiàngimpression
轮廓lúnkuòoutline
Memory tip: if a lesson contains several words about what is seen, collect them into one “appearance/perception” group.
What is the most useful learning link among 镜子, 面孔, 印象, and 轮廓?

4. Contrast pair: 天堂 ↔ 地狱

Strong contrast pairs are especially useful because they sharpen meaning through opposition.

天堂tiāntángheaven
地狱dìyùhell
尘世chénshìmortal world
高贵gāoguìnoble
Memory tip: one useful method is to remember a concept through its opposite. 天堂 and 地狱 create a clear semantic contrast that supports related words like 尘世.
Why is 天堂 useful to study together with 地狱?
