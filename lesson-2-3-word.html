Focus on Patterns

Reliable clues only: fewer, but more accurate

1. 扌 often signals hand action

This is a relatively stable clue in this lesson. The hand component 扌 often points to an action done with the hand or body.

挑tiāocarry on shoulder
打dǎhit / hold / roll
打伞dǎ sǎnhold an umbrella
打滚儿dǎgǔnrroll about
Memory tip: when you see 扌, first ask whether the word involves a physical action done by hand. It is a clue, not a perfect rule.
What is the safest character clue in 挑水, 打伞, and 打滚儿?

2. Eye-and-tears meaning network

Here the most reliable link is semantic, not radical. These words belong to one clear meaning field: seeing, crying, and visible emotional response.

眼角yǎnjiǎocorner of the eye
眼泪yǎnlèitears
落泪luò lèiweep
叹息tànxīsigh
Memory tip: some words should be remembered as a scene: eye → tears → weeping → sighing. This is more accurate here than forcing a shared-radical explanation.
Why is it useful to study 眼角, 眼泪, 落泪, and 叹息 together?

3. Social response contrast: 同情 vs 嘲笑

Strong contrasts help students organize meaning clearly. This lesson gives a very useful opposition between compassion and ridicule.

同情tóngqíngsympathize
安慰ānwèicomfort
慰问wèiwènshow sympathy
嘲笑cháoxiàomock
Memory tip: when you meet a new social-emotion word, ask whether it belongs to the “care/support” side or the “ridicule/harm” side.
What is the strongest learning relationship between 同情 and 嘲笑?

4. Rough living-conditions network

Another reliable pattern in this lesson is a scene-based network: dirty space, insects, biting, holes, darkness, and damage.

猪窝zhūwōpigsty
臭虫chòuchóngbedbug
窟窿kūlonghole
湿shīwet
Memory tip: when a lesson gives many unpleasant concrete nouns, build one environment in your mind. Scene memory is often stronger than isolated memorization.
Why is 猪窝 useful to study together with 臭虫, 窟窿, and 湿?
