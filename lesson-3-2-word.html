Focus on Patterns

Learn through radicals, components, and category words

1. 火字旁 / 火字底：火、热、煮、燃

In this lesson, the most teachable visual pattern is 火 and its bottom form 灬. It often points to burning, heating, cooking, or intense transformation. This is especially useful for the imagery of 煮豆燃萁.

煮zhǔto boil
燃ránto burn
煎jiānto fry / simmer
然ránform clue
热rèheat
Memory tip: when students see 火 or 灬, ask: “Is there fire, heat, cooking, or transformation here?” In this lesson, 煮, 燃, and 煎 can be remembered together as a strong mini-network built around heat.
In Lesson 3.2, what is the most useful meaning clue carried by 火字旁 or 火字底?

2. 月字旁：从 同胞 进去，往“身体”展开

This lesson has fewer direct 月-radical words, so the best anchor is 胞 in 同胞. In many modern characters, 月字旁 helps students think of the body, flesh, or an inner physical connection.

胞bāobody / womb link
同胞tóngbāosame-mother siblings
肚dùbelly
脸liǎnface
腿tuǐleg
Memory tip: in this lesson, teach 月字旁 through 同胞: same body, same mother, same source. Then expand outward to other body-related words. That makes the radical feel meaningful instead of random.
Why is 同胞 a good anchor for teaching 月字旁 in Lesson 3.2?

3. 立刀旁 / 刂：分开、裁断、离别

The radical 刂 often carries a sense of cutting, dividing, separating, or making a distinction. In this lesson, the most practical entry point is 别 in 临别, because parting is a kind of separation.

别biéseparate / other
临别línbiéat parting
告别gàobiésay farewell
分别fēnbiéseparate / distinguish
判pànjudge
Memory tip: even when the original “knife” meaning is not literal, 刂 often helps build a semantic bridge toward division, separation, distinction, or decision. That is why 临别 is such a teachable lesson anchor.
In Lesson 3.2, what is the best way to explain the learning value of 立刀旁 / 刂?

4. “家” 的词：不是“家里”，而是“某一类专家”

In advanced Chinese, 家 after a field noun often marks an accomplished specialist, thinker, or expert. In this lesson, 军事家 and 文学家 are especially useful because they help students move from literal meaning to academic categorization.

军事家jūnshìjiāstrategist
文学家wénxuéjiāman of letters
艺术家yìshùjiāartist
科学家kēxuéjiāscientist
思想家sīxiǎngjiāthinker
Memory tip: teach students that 家 here does not mainly mean “home.” It works like a category marker for an accomplished person in a field. Once they understand that, 军事家 and 文学家 become much easier to decode.
In 军事家 and 文学家, what does 家 most usefully signal?
